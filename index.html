About Me

Hello! My name is Larsen Weigle. Welcome to my personal website. As I'm not a seasoned web developer, I decided to improve my skills by crafting this website from scratch. It's a work in progress, and I plan to add more advanced features in the future.

I'm a dedicated computer science student at Stanford University, specializing in artificial intelligence with a focus on natural language processing and large language models. I graduated with my Bachelor's degree in June 2023 and I am now pursuing my Master's degree, which I aim to complete by June 2024.

My passion has always been to leverage technology for impactful change, which is why my interest lies particularly at the intersection of AI and climate change research.

Currently, I'm gaining hands-on experience with open-source large language models as a Machine Learning Intern at Caktus AI. Previously, I served as a Software Engineer Intern at Moda AI, where I developed custom scripts to enhance the performance of a novel CLIP embedding model. You can find more information about my experiences in my resume, linked below.

Beyond the world of computer science, I have a profound love for marine biology and surfing. In my free time, you'll often find me at some of my favorite Northern California beaches, like Montara State Beach, spending time with friends.

I'm always eager to contribute to new projects, especially those in the realm of climate tech. Feel free to reach out to me at weigle23@stanford.edu to discuss potential collaborations or opportunities.

Thank you for taking the time to learn more about me. I look forward to connecting with you!
